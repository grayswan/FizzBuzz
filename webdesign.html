web design: past, present, and future

by: Kristen Swan-Grashel

Like pretty much everyone around in the 90's, I remember the early days of web design well: terrible graphics, ugly color combinations, and boring fonts. Back then, I didn't know any better. I was just happy to be exploring this cool new thing called the internet. By the time a page finally loaded, I didn't care what it looked like. I was just happy to be there.



Thankfully, web design has reached new, beautiful heights, where form and function unite to produce easily navigable, visually rich websites. And as someone who is aesthetically-driven, I couldn't be happier to be coming into web development at this time.



I've enjoyed watching web development change over the last few years. Mobile, in particular has come a long way, but I have also noticed great strides in other areas such as online retail. For example online retailer, Bonlook allows the shopper to upload a picture and virtually try on a pair of glasses before buying them. And that's one of the directions I see the web heading: towards an increasingly interactive user experience.
